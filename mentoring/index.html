How do we get a participant to return?

It's important that participants return because...

We can do that by...

  • make sure people feel welcome
  • match tasks to participants, ensure they are successful the first time
  • keep people engaged even when they struggle
  • help without being overbearing
  • enable folks to continue afterward on their own
  • say hi in irc

Where do mentors come from?

Engage new contributors and experienced developers
alike to participate in mentoring

That is how mentors can help bring in people to participate, and become repeat participants. But where do the mentors come from? This is one of the keys to mentoring in public. Experienced contributors will see the mentor interactions and reuse those patterns themselves when helping people. They can also see what mentors really do, and will see they can be a mentor. (Dont have to know answers to questions, only share how to find answers.) Ask in advance for mentors, explain the "qualifications" (minimal qualifications and there will be support for mentors). Explain the expectations. Ask in a pinch. If someone has done a task a few times, and a new person wants help doing it, ask that experienced person to help. Now they are a mentor.

Support mentors

instead of doing this for tasks the participants do, do it for the mentors

  • continue to be a resource

  • participate side by side
  • letting new people make mistakes (10 second rule)
